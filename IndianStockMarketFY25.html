Performance of Indian Stock Market FY 2024-25
By Lakshman Surampudi, Ch Akshitha & Amit Jena | December 07, 2025
Python Power BI Data Analysis Data Visualization Financial Analysis

Introduction

The Indian stock market, with its dynamic, complex, and highly volatile nature, presents a perfect subject for end-to-end data analysis. As a key indicator of economic health, investor sentiment, and policy impacts, understanding its movements requires sophisticated statistical and data science techniques. This project represents our team's first step into data journalism, aiming to navigate the audience through the overwhelming yet fascinating journey of India's stock market during FY 2024-25.

This comprehensive analysis was created keeping in mind both general audiences curious about the stock market and data professionals who appreciate data-driven storytelling. Our goal is to review and explain what happened in this turbulent year through compelling visualizations and insightful analysis.

Project Objective & Scope

The central question driving this project is: "Is the annual performance of the Indian stock market during FY 2024-25 good or bad?" This seemingly simple question requires a multi-dimensional analysis, as the answer varies based on numerous factors and perspectives.

To answer this comprehensively, we broke down our analysis into six key areas:

  • Performance of primary indices (BSE SENSEX, NSE NIFTY50) in terms of market cap and index price values
  • Sectoral and segment-wise analysis identifying bullish and bearish trends
  • Market breadth tracking through Advances-Decliners ratio
  • Impact of macroeconomic indicators and global events
  • Institutional sentiment analysis (FII and DII investments)
  • IPO statistics revealing business activity encouragement

Methodology & Data Collection

This end-to-end data analysis project followed a structured approach: defining goals, collecting data from authorized sources (NSE, BSE, SEBI) and reliable financial platforms, cleaning and transforming data using Python and Excel, conducting exploratory analysis, and creating compelling visualizations in Power BI.

Key Challenge: Data from various sources came in different formats and with inconsistencies. We implemented an ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline, using text processing techniques in Python to standardize entries, particularly for IPO data that required merging from three different sources.

Executive Summary: FY 2024-25 at a Glance

Figure 1: Executive Summary Dashboard showing key market indicators

Benchmark Indices Performance

The year showed modest growth for benchmark indices, though significantly lower than the previous year:

  • BSE SENSEX: Closed at 77,414.92 points (March 28, 2025), gaining 3,763 points for a 5.11% growth (compared to 24.85% in FY 2023-24)
  • NIFTY50: Finished at 23,519.35 points, gaining 1,192 points with 5.34% growth (compared to 28.61% in FY 2023-24)

Macroeconomic Environment

India's economic fundamentals remained relatively stable throughout FY 2024-25:

  • GDP Growth: Steady at 6.2% (compared to 6.5% previous year)
  • GDP per Capita: Rose from $2.7K to $2.9K, indicating rising income levels
  • Inflation: Controlled at 4.2%, demonstrating successful monetary policy
  • Fiscal Deficit: Improved to 4.9% of GDP from 5.6% previous year
  • Repo Rate: Maintained at 6.5% through most of the year, with a slight reduction to 6.3% in February 2025

Global Investment Sentiment

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) showed significant risk aversion, particularly during October-February, resulting in net outflows. However, Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs) stepped up, compensating for FII outflows and neutralizing negative effects. This domestic resilience became a crucial stabilizing factor for the market.

Market Breadth Concerns

The Advances-Decliners Ratio (ADR) revealed a worrying trend: an observable downward trajectory throughout the year indicated that decliners increased while advances decreased. This suggests that while benchmark indices showed modest gains, the broader market struggled, with fewer stocks participating in the upward movement.

Sectoral Performance: Winners and Losers

Top Performing Sectors (YTD)

  • Healthcare: The star performer with 12.25% gain (NIFTY) and 16.77% gain (SENSEX)
  • Banking: Strong performance with 10.47% growth in BSE Bank, despite inherent short-term volatility. P/E ratio of just 14.32 suggests reasonable valuation
  • IT Sector: Remained resilient with steady gains throughout the year

Underperforming Sectors

  • Energy: The biggest loser with -14.99% decline on NIFTY. Started at 40,000 points and ended at 33,682, losing about 6,400 points despite a profitable first half
  • Realty: Severe losses of -9.43% (NIFTY) and -10.86% (SENSEX). BSE Realty fell from 7,405 to 6,601 points, with particularly sharp collapses in late January and March
  • Auto Sector: BSE Auto suffered a 2.68% loss despite strong Q1 and Q2 performance
Valuation Alert: Most indices showed concerning P/E and P/B ratios indicating overvaluation. NIFTY50 (P/E: 21.37, P/B: 3.48) and SENSEX (P/E: 23.20, P/B: 4.05) both suggest elevated valuations. BSE Healthcare's P/E of 43.67 and BSE Realty's P/E of 60.8 are particularly high, warranting caution from investors.

Segment Performance

  • BSE: Mid-cap outperformed with 6.22% growth, though showing a concerning downward quarterly trend (Q1: +16.4%, Q4: -10%)
  • NSE: Small-cap showed better returns compared to large-cap and mid-cap segments, though with notably higher volatility

Retail Investment: Growing Participation

FY 2024-25 witnessed a significant rise in retail participation, driven by:

  • Growing awareness among retail investors, especially young workforce
  • Widespread internet penetration and instant news access
  • Eased regulations and encouraging incentives from SEBI (such as T+0 settlement cycle)
  • Steady increase in demat account holdings
  • Rising mutual fund investments and IPO subscription rates

Conclusion & Future Outlook

FY 2024-25 presented a year of subdued growth for the Indian stock market. While benchmark indices (SENSEX and NIFTY50) managed modest gains of around 5%, this represents a significant slowdown from the previous year's 24-28% growth. The year was characterized by a tale of two halves: strong performance in Q1 and Q2 followed by substantial losses in Q3 and marginal declines in Q4.

The downward trend in the Advances-Decliners Ratio coupled with significant sectoral divergence (Healthcare and Banking thriving while Energy and Realty suffered) paints a picture of a market under stress. Foreign institutional investor outflows during October-February added to the pressure, though domestic institutional investors provided crucial support.

Silver Linings:
  • Stable macroeconomic fundamentals (6.2% GDP growth, controlled inflation at 4.2%)
  • Strong retail participation and IPO market enthusiasm
  • Market resurgence in March 2025 providing hope for recovery
  • Resilient domestic institutional support compensating for foreign outflows

The market resurgence in the final month of the financial year—reflected in index recovery, improved ADR, and renewed FII investments—offers consolation and hope. This late-year momentum suggests potential for brighter prospects in FY 2025-26, though caution is warranted given elevated valuations across most indices.

Overall, despite geopolitical tensions and global headwinds, the Indian stock market demonstrated resilience underpinned by strong domestic fundamentals and adaptive investor behavior. The journey through FY 2024-25 serves as a reminder of the market's inherent volatility and the importance of diversified participation, robust investor education, and long-term perspective in navigating equity investments.
